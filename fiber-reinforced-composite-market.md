<p><p>The fiber reinforced composite market comprises two main types: thermoplastic and thermosetting composites. Thermoplastic composites are characterized by their ability to be remolded upon heating, offering increased design flexibility and recyclability. They typically provide better impact resistance and processing speeds. In contrast, thermosetting composites cure irreversibly when heated, resulting in stronger and more heat-resistant materials, ideal for high-performance applications. Both types are utilized across industries such as aerospace, automotive, and construction, driven by the demand for lightweight and durable materials.</p></p>
