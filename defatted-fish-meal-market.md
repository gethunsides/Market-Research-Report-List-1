<p><p>Defatted fish meal is a high-protein feed ingredient derived from fish processing, with two primary market types: flame-dried (FD) and steam-dried (SD) fish meal. FD fish meal involves rapid drying using direct heat, resulting in a distinctive flavor and higher nutrient retention, making it suitable for animal feed. In contrast, SD fish meal employs steam heat for a more controlled drying process, which can lead to improved digestibility and reduced oxidation, appealing to aquaculture and livestock sectors prioritizing quality and safety.</p></p>
